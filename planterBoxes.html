Planter Boxes

This was my Eagle Scout Project for the Menlo Park VA Hospital. I oversaw more than 10 other scouts in this role, with the total project taking over 100 man hours

I approached the Menlo Park VA because I knew I wanted my Eagle Project to benefit our veterans. They told me that many of their residents love to garden, but are wheelchair-bound and have difficulty doing so. So, I designed and constructed three wheelchair-accessible planter boxes.


The boxes were constructed out of redwood with a plywood sheet bottom for added strength and cost. The inside was also sealed with caulk and then covered in plastic sheet, with a spigot attached for drainage. Finally, the boxes were mounted on wheels, sanded, and stained.



This was a really fun project and great learning experience for me. I got to help people I care about while also woodworking, something that I've enjoyed ever since building Pinewood Derby cars as a Cub Scout. In addition, I got to plan a project and oversee a large group of people to complete it over a several-month timespan. This was invaluable experience that is hard to come by at such a young age.
